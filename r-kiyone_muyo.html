Kiyone Muyo

By Thrythlind Hardwulf (thrythlind@yahoo.com)

Season 1 : "Return of the Akuneko"

  • Episode 1 : "No Need for Mysteries"
    A stranger crosses a check point on the far edges of Jurai territory. Ryoko sees someoneat Kiyone's and Mihoshi's latest work place that she immediately tries to avoid. The encounter seems to have awakened some of Ryoko's memories. 14KB -6/21/2000

  • Episode 2 : "No Need for Questions"
    Rei Kuroi battles Nagi on his way to Earth. Back at the Masaki residence Ryoko sneaks out early in the morning and returns home exhausted. Once there she has to face Kiyone's questions about her behavior the day before. 17KB -6/21/2000
  • Episode 3 : "No Need for Assassins"
    Nagi sends a warning to Kiyone about Rei, who finally reaches Earth. While examining one of his artifacts, Rikyu accidentally activates it and sees the arrival of Rei's craft. 14KB -6/21/2000
  • Episode 4 : "No Need for a Battle"
    Rei attacks Rikyu and is winning when the Masaki household intervenes. He flees and most of the warriors take to space to catch him. After some maneuvering the battle insues. 19KB -6/21/2000
  • Episode 5 : "No Need for a Prisoner"
    A bit of humor before I get into the serious matter opened up at the end of No Need for a Battle. 15KB -6/21/2000
  • Episode 6 : "No Need for a Family"
    Ryoko and Rikyu finally talk about the fact revealed in No Need for a Battle. Kiyone interrogates Rei and discovers a disturbing fact. Fortunately some new help arrives. 16KB -6/21/2000
  • Episode 7 : "No Need for Preparations"
    The Masaki household split up to begin preparing for the war they believe is coming. While they are all involved in their various activites Rei dreams about offers of power from someone he has never seen before, but is well known to the Tenchi crew. 17KB -6/21/2000
  • Episode 8 : "No Need for Command"
    The first sign of the Akuneko forces appears on two fronts, the larger of the two pressing for Earth. Kiyone is given command of the Sol system defenses and illustrates her plan to welcome the approaching fleet. 16KB -6/21/2000
  • Episode 9 : "No Need for an Ambush"
    The Akuneko forces on the Jurai vector are crushed. Jurai redistributes forces to send the vastly outnumbered Sol System forces much needed aid. Meanwhile, Kiyone's plan unfolds. 15KB -6/21/2000
  • Episode 10 : "No Need for a Melee"
    The cabbits change tactics, and Washu is forced to shut off her device when her station is attacked. In the heat of the battle Lord Ryoi and Kiyone have a chat via holograph. 19KB -6/21/2000
  • Episode 11 : "No Need for Medics"
    Kiyone begins to acquire a small following, one of whom informs her something of current Akuneko politics. Ryoko has a sense of danger to one of her friends while Kiyone and her group help out at with the overflow casualties at Tenchi's clinic. 15KB -6/21/2000
  • Episode 12 : "No Need for Talk"
    As the surrender talks begin, Ryoko seems angry at Tenchi. Lord Ryoi's plots against his own people are revealed by his own arrogance. He and some followers try to flee, but it appears they are destroyed. 17KB -6/21/2000

Season 2 : "Home Life and Shadow Plays"

  • Episode 1 : "No Need for a Home"
    Rikyu, Kiyone and Mihoshi go house hunting. Back at the Galaxy Police Academy, an old classmate of Kiyone's trains a familiar new cadet. 14KB -6/21/2000
  • Episode 2 : "No Need for Cameos"
    Ayeka gets tired of dealing with Ryoko's over-protectiveness, Tenchi's guilt. She takes a trip with Ryo-Ohki into the Nerima district and discusses her problems with two girls whose love problems could make hers look simple. 15KB -6/21/2000
  • Episode 3 : "No Need for Renovation"
    In the process of cleaning and fixxing the new Miho-Kiyo place, Mihoshi discovers a hidden passage way and gets lost in them. Kiyone and Rikyu follow to look for her, but get side-tracked. 20KB -6/21/2000
  • Episode 4 : "No Need for a Movie"
    Ayeka suddenly suggests that they all go out to a movie together. While not opposed to the idea, the Tenchi crew do notice that Ayeka is acting a little strangely. The movie chosen by Ayeka elicits some reminiscing by the crew, especially Ryoko and Tenchi. 17KB -6/21/2000
  • Episode 5 : "No Need for Argument"
    A sniper takes aim at a member of the Tenchi crew as they exit the theatre. After a brief, one-sided argument over jurisdiction with the local police, everybody goes off to track down Ryoko and Tenchi. 16KB -6/21/2000
  • Episode 6 : "No Need for Lawyers"
    Kiyone reads the sniper his rights, and he requests a lawyer. He is beginning to think that it won't help him much after he hear's about Ryoko's previous jail time. Ayeka and Tenchi officially break-up, leaving Ryoko dumbfounded. 12KB -6/21/2000
  • Episode 7 : "No Need for Conspiracies"
    The assassin meets his lawyer, the lone survivor of the GPs legal department from the Caine incident, and decides to tell Kiyone everything she wants to here. Mihoshi and Sasami find a neat new game made by Washu inside the Yagami's computer banks. 12KB -6/21/2000
  • Episode 8 : "No Need for Apologies"
    Ryoko and Tenchi have a sparring a match that leads to deeper things while Washu runs Ayeka through some more tests. 13KB -6/21/2000
  • Episode 9 : "No Need for a Cure"
    Washu discovers a possible cure for Ayeka. The survivors of the akuneko assault on Jurai relay a request to Ryoko through the knight Kamidake. 10KB -6/21/2000
  • Episode 10 : "No Need for Diplomacy"
    Kiyone, Tenchi and Ryoko go to tell SETI about themselves as a first step in letting Earth no about them being around. Rakkan and Karya come up with a way to keep Mihoshi away during the soap operas. 12KB -6/21/2000
